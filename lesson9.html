Lesson 9
Follow-Up Coach
Draft better replies and next steps without sounding robotic

The Problem With Follow-Up

Follow-up fails in two common ways. You wait too long, or you send a message that sounds generic.

Your agent can help with both, but only if you give it the context from Lesson 8.

Today you are building a Follow-Up Coach that turns captured opportunities into useful messages.

The Tool You Are Building

Your Follow-Up Coach should help you decide what to say, when to say it, and what outcome you want.

  1. Intent -- why you are reaching out
  2. Context -- what they said or needed
  3. Tone -- friendly, direct, warm, professional
  4. Ask -- the clear next step
  5. Version -- text, email, LinkedIn, or call notes
Step 1

Install the Follow-Up Coach Prompt

Copy this into your agent.

Act as my Follow-Up Coach. When I paste an opportunity, help me create a follow-up message. Ask me for any missing details, then draft: 1. A short text message 2. A slightly longer email 3. A call note version 4. The clear next step I am asking for 5. One softer version if the first draft feels too direct Keep the message human. No hype. No fake urgency. No corporate language.
Step 2

Use One Captured Opportunity

Paste the opportunity you captured in Lesson 8. If you skipped Lesson 8, use the template below.

Use this opportunity and draft a follow-up. Person or company: [name] Context: [how we connected] Need or interest: [what they care about] Important detail: [detail to mention] Desired next step: [call, reply, intro, proposal, meeting, reminder] Tone: [friendly, direct, warm, professional] Channel: [text, email, LinkedIn, phone note] Draft the message and explain why it works.
Step 3

Pick the Right Follow-Up Type

They asked for informationSend the answer plus one next stepRemoves friction
They went quietShort check-in with an easy replyLowers pressure
You owe them somethingDeliver first, then askBuilds trust
They need timePermission-based reminderRespects timing
You are unsureAsk for a simple yes or noCreates clarity
Step 4

Make It Sound Like You

The first draft is rarely the final draft. Make your agent learn your voice.

Rewrite this follow-up in my voice. Rules: - Keep it short - Make it sound like a real person - Remove hype - Remove stiff corporate phrasing - Keep the next step clear - Give me 3 versions: casual, direct, and warm Message to rewrite: [paste message]

What's Happening Behind the Scenes

You are moving from "write this for me" to a repeatable communication workflow.

Bad UseBetter Use
Write a follow-upUse this context, goal, tone, and next step
Make it professionalMake it clear, human, and specific
Say moreSay only what helps the person act
Step 5

Your Assignment

Your to-do list:
1. Pick one real opportunity
2. Draft a follow-up using the coach prompt
3. Ask for three versions
4. Pick one and edit it yourself
5. Save the final message and next step

The Bigger Picture

Good follow-up is not pressure. It is clarity. Your agent helps you keep promises, make next steps visible, and sound more like yourself.

The real takeaway: Follow-up works best when it is specific, timely, and easy to answer.
