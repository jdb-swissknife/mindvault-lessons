Lesson 3
Video Briefs
Turn long videos into quick business takeaways

The Problem With Video Content

There's more business content on YouTube than anyone can watch. Webinars, interviews, competitor deep-dives, industry panels, training videos. Some of it is genuinely useful.

But here's what actually happens:

  • You save a video to "watch later" (you never do)
  • You start a 45-minute interview and skim for the good part (you miss it)
  • Someone sends you a "must-watch" link (it sits in your inbox for two weeks)
  • You watch the whole thing and can't remember the key points a day later

You don't have a video problem. You have a time problem. The information in those videos is valuable. The format just doesn't work for a busy schedule.

The Solution

Your agent now has a skill called Video Briefs. Here's how it works:

  1. Send a link -- Paste any YouTube URL into your agent chat
  2. Wait a minute -- Your agent watches it so you don't have to
  3. Get the brief -- A clean summary with the parts that matter for your business

No more saving videos for later. No more sitting through fluff. You get the useful parts fast.

Step 1

Try Your First Video Brief

Find a YouTube video you've been meaning to watch -- a webinar, an interview, a competitor's content, anything. Then paste the link into your agent like this:

Here's a video I've been meaning to watch. Can you give me a brief on it? https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EXAMPLE_VIDEO_ID Include: - A quick summary - The key takeaways - Action steps I should consider - Why this matters for my business

Your agent will process the video and return a structured brief with four sections:

SectionWhat You Get
SummaryThe main points in plain language, 3-5 sentences
Key TakeawaysThe ideas worth remembering, numbered and clear
Action StepsSpecific things you could do based on the content
Business RelevanceWhy this matters for YOUR business, not just generic notes
Pro tip: The more context your agent has about your business, the better the "Business Relevance" section gets. If you've been using your agent for ideas and subscriptions (Lessons 1 and 2), it already knows your industry, your challenges, and your goals. That context makes the brief way more useful than generic YouTube summaries.
Step 2

Ways to Use Video Briefs

This isn't just a time-saver. It's a research tool. Here are the ways business owners use it most:

Competitor Research

"Can you brief this video from [competitor]? I want to know what they're telling our potential customers."

Industry Updates

"Here's a panel discussion from the latest industry conference. Pull out the trends I should pay attention to."

Team Training

"Brief this training video for me. I want to know if it's worth having my whole team watch it or if I can just share the highlights."

Sales Prep

"This is a podcast interview with the CEO of a company I'm pitching next week. What do I need to know?"

Founder Education

"Here's a long interview with a founder who's a few steps ahead of me. What lessons apply to where I am right now?"
Step 3

Follow-Up Questions

The brief is just the start. Once your agent has processed a video, you can dig deeper without re-watching anything:

Get more detail on one point:

"In that video brief, tell me more about point 3. What exactly did they say about pricing?"

Compare across videos:

"I've sent you three competitor videos this month. What patterns do you see across all of them?"

Turn insights into action:

"Based on this brief, draft a to-do list for me. What should I tackle first?"

Save for later reference:

"Save this brief. Tag it as 'competitor research' and remind me about it in my weekly review."

Step 4

Your Assignment

Your to-do list:
1. Find a YouTube video you've been saving for later (you know you have one)
2. Paste the link into your agent with "Can you brief this video?"
3. Read through the brief -- summary, takeaways, actions, relevance
4. Ask one follow-up question about something in the brief
5. Say "Save this brief and tag it" so you can find it later

Once you see a 45-minute video turned into a one-page brief with action steps, you'll start sending every video to your agent.

The Bigger Picture

Lesson 1 taught you that your agent remembers and reminds.

Lesson 2 taught you that your agent thinks with you and that you can teach it new skills.

Lesson 3 adds a new capability: your agent can process information for you.

  • You don't have to read every article -- send it to your agent
  • You don't have to watch every video -- get the brief instead
  • You don't have to sort through noise -- your agent gives you what matters
The real takeaway: Less watching. Faster decisions.

Your time is the most expensive thing in your business. Every minute you spend watching content that could have been a brief is a minute you could have spent on something only you can do.
