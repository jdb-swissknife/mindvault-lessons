Lesson 18
Audience Intelligence Engine
Research business ideas before you spend time, money, or reputation

The Problem With Business Ideas

Most business ideas start in your head. That is normal. You notice a problem, see a gap, hear a customer complain, or spot something your market might want.

The risky part is what happens next.

  • You build the offer before you know if people care
  • You write copy before you know what words customers already use
  • You price it before you know what alternatives they compare it to
  • You ask friends for opinions instead of looking for market evidence

A good idea should survive contact with the market before it gets your time, money, or reputation.

The Solution

The Audience Intelligence Engine helps you research a business idea through real public signals. It is built for the middle of your first month because you now have enough agent habits to use it the right way.

  1. Start with a rough idea -- Tell your agent what you are considering
  2. Sharpen the audience -- Your agent helps turn the idea into a clear research question
  3. Run the research -- Use the Audience Intelligence Engine to collect market signals
  4. Bring the report back -- Paste the report into your agent for analysis
  5. Choose the next test -- Decide whether to pursue, adjust, or pause
The rule: Do not ask your agent, "Is this a good idea?" Ask your agent to find evidence.
Step 1

Turn Your Idea Into a Research Question

Do not start by typing random keywords into a search box. Start by giving your agent the idea and the audience. Let it help you tighten the research target first.

I have a business idea I want to research before I build it. Idea: [describe the idea] Target audience: [who it is for] Help me turn this into a clear research question for the Audience Intelligence Engine. I want to know what this audience already wants, fears, complains about, buys, and ignores.

Your agent should help you turn a fuzzy idea into a sharper research frame:

Instead ofResearch
"AI for roofers"What admin work do roofing owners complain about losing time to?
"A course for new managers"What first-time managers are embarrassed to admit they struggle with?
"A new local service"What do homeowners already ask for, compare, and distrust?
Step 2

Run the Audience Intelligence Engine

Open the tool and run the research using the audience and question your agent helped you create.

Open Audience Intelligence Engine

The tool is designed to help surface:

  • Desires -- what the audience wants more of
  • Pain -- what they complain about or avoid
  • Objections -- what stops them from buying
  • Language -- the words they already use
  • Alternatives -- what they use instead of your idea
  • Buying triggers -- what might make them act now
Privacy note: Treat this like research, not a place for secrets. Do not paste passwords, private client data, or anything sensitive into a research tool.
Step 3

Bring the Report Back to Your Agent

The tool gives you raw research. Your agent turns that research into judgment. Paste the report back into your agent and ask for a decision-focused analysis.

I used the Audience Intelligence Engine and pasted the report below. Report: [paste report] Analyze it for me. Give me: 1. The strongest demand signals 2. The biggest objections 3. The exact phrases customers use 4. Competitors or alternatives they already use 5. A simple test I can run this week 6. A recommendation: pursue, adjust, or pause

Step 4

Compare Ideas Without Getting Attached

The most useful part of agentic research is not proving your idea is right. It is finding out which idea deserves the next test.

I have research reports for three business ideas. Compare them and rank them from strongest to weakest based on: - clear customer pain - willingness to pay - ease of reaching the audience - competitive pressure - speed to test Do not flatter the ideas. Be direct.
Owner mindset: You are not looking for compliments. You are looking for signal. The faster a weak idea gets exposed, the less it costs you.

Step 5

Your Assignment

Your to-do list:

2. Ask your agent to sharpen the audience and research question

4. Run the research

The Bigger Picture

By this point in the journey, your agent is becoming more than a helper. It is becoming a research loop.

  • You bring the idea
  • Your agent sharpens the question
  • The tool gathers signals
  • Your agent turns the signals into a decision
  • You run a small test before making a big bet
The real takeaway: Better research leads to smaller mistakes.

You do not need to guess less because you have no instinct. You need to guess less because your time is expensive.
