Lesson 8
Lead Capture: Build Your Opportunity Tracker
Track people, context, and follow-ups before they slip away

The Problem With "I'll Remember"

Most opportunities do not disappear because the idea was bad. They disappear because the context was never captured.

A person mentions a need. A customer asks a question. A friend shares a contact. You think, "I will follow up later." Then the details fade.

Today you are building a lightweight Opportunity Tracker with your agent.

The Tool You Are Building

This tracker works for business leads, job opportunities, project collaborators, referral partners, or anyone you need to follow up with.

  1. Person -- who they are
  2. Context -- where the opportunity came from
  3. Need -- what they care about
  4. Next step -- what should happen next
  5. Follow-up date -- when to come back to it
Step 1

Create the Capture Format

Copy this into your agent to define the format you want it to use.

I want to build a simple Opportunity Tracker. When I say "capture opportunity," ask me for: 1. Person or company 2. How I met them or where this came from 3. What they need or care about 4. Important details to remember 5. Next step 6. Follow-up date 7. Priority: low, medium, or high After I answer, summarize it in a clean table row and ask if I want to save it.
Step 2

Capture One Real Opportunity

Pick someone real. It can be a sales lead, a networking contact, a friend you owe a reply, or a possible project partner.

Capture opportunity. Person or company: [name] Source: [where this came from] Need or interest: [what they care about] Details: [important context] Next step: [what I should do next] Follow-up date: [date] Priority: [low, medium, or high] Turn this into a clean Opportunity Tracker entry.
Step 3

Choose Your Follow-Up Rule

A tracker is only useful if it creates movement. Pick a simple rule.

Opportunity TypeSuggested Rule
Hot leadFollow up within 24 hours
Warm relationshipFollow up within 3 days
Interesting ideaReview during weekly planning
Referral partnerSend value before asking for anything
Personal commitmentSet a date and keep it visible
Step 4

Ask for a Weekly Opportunity Review

This turns the tracker into a repeatable tool instead of a one-time note.

Each week, help me review my Opportunity Tracker. For each open opportunity, show: - person or company - priority - last context - next step - follow-up date - suggested message or action Start with the highest priority and oldest follow-ups first.

What's Happening Behind the Scenes

You are building your first small relationship system. It is not a full CRM. It is a practical memory-backed tracker for real life.

Simple standard: If it involves another person and a next step, capture it.
Step 5

Your Assignment

Your to-do list:
1. Save the Opportunity Tracker format
2. Capture one real opportunity
3. Give it a priority and follow-up date
4. Ask your agent what the next step should be
5. Save the final entry where you can find it again

The Bigger Picture

This is how your agent starts helping with momentum. Not by guessing who matters, but by keeping people, context, and next steps from disappearing.

The real takeaway: A captured opportunity is easier to act on than a remembered one.
