Lesson 7
Week 1 Review: Build Your Agent Field Report
Turn your first six lessons into one practical operating snapshot

The Problem With Learning Without Capture

You have already tried memory, idea capture, video briefs, daily commands, tool requests, and safety rules.

That is useful, but it can disappear if you do not turn it into a simple working document.

Today you are building your first Agent Field Report. It is a one-page summary of what your agent now knows, what you built, and what you want next.

The Tool You Are Building

Your Week 1 Field Report becomes the first page in your personal operating kit.

  1. What my agent knows -- preferences, goals, active projects, safety rules
  2. What I can ask for now -- useful commands you have already tested
  3. What I want to build next -- one workflow request from Lesson 5
  4. What I should not skip -- safety reminders from Lesson 6
Quest logic: A real agent owner does not just collect prompts. They keep a living map of what the agent can do.
Step 1

Ask Your Agent for a Field Report

Copy this into your agent. If you have not completed every earlier lesson, answer with what you do have.

Create my Week 1 Agent Field Report. Use what you know about me from our work so far. If something is missing, ask me one question at a time. Include these sections: 1. My top goals right now 2. Preferences you should remember 3. Active projects or ideas 4. Useful commands I have learned this week 5. My safety rules 6. One workflow or tool I want to build next 7. The smallest useful next step Keep it practical. Write it like a field report I can save and reuse.

Step 3

Save the Field Report

Once the report looks useful, ask your agent to save the durable parts.

Save the durable parts of this Week 1 Field Report. Remember only the parts that will help you work with me in future sessions: - stable preferences - active project facts - recurring goals - safety rules - the next workflow I want to build Do not save temporary notes that will be stale next week.
