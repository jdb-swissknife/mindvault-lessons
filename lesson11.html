Lesson 11
Expense Notes
Capture spending in plain English before details disappear

The Problem With Money Details

Receipts, subscriptions, tools, mileage, supplies, meals, trial accounts, client purchases. The spending happens fast. The notes do not.

You do not need a full accounting system today. You need a simple habit for capturing context while it is fresh.

Today you are building an Expense Notes workflow with your agent.

The Tool You Are Building

This is a plain-English capture tool. It helps you remember what you bought, why you bought it, and what category it likely belongs in.

  1. Amount -- what was spent
  2. Vendor -- where it went
  3. Purpose -- why it happened
  4. Category -- suggested bucket
  5. Follow-up -- cancel, reimburse, attach receipt, review later
Important: This is not tax or legal advice. It is a capture habit so you have cleaner records to review with the right professional if needed.
Step 1

Create Your Expense Capture Prompt

Copy this into your agent.

I want to build an Expense Notes workflow. When I say "capture expense," ask me for: 1. Amount 2. Vendor 3. Date 4. What it was for 5. Project, client, or personal use 6. Whether I have a receipt 7. Any follow-up needed Then turn it into a clean expense note with a suggested category and a confidence level.
Step 2

Capture One Real Expense

Use a harmless example first. A software subscription, office supply, learning tool, mileage note, or business meal works well.

Capture expense. Amount: [$] Vendor: [vendor] Date: [date] Purpose: [why I bought it] Project or client: [if relevant] Receipt: [yes, no, need to find] Follow-up needed: [cancel trial, attach receipt, ask for reimbursement, review later, none] Create a clean expense note and suggest a category.
Step 3

Set Your Categories

Start simple. You can refine later.

CategoryExamplesFollow-Up Question

LearningCourses, books, communitiesWhat outcome should it support?
SalesTravel, meals, demos, outreach toolsWhich opportunity does it support?
OperationsSupplies, admin, workspace itemsShould it be repeated or avoided?
Client or projectPurchases tied to a specific jobDoes it need reimbursement or billing?
Step 4

Build the Weekly Review

Expense capture becomes valuable when you review it.

At the end of each week, help me review my Expense Notes. Show me: 1. Total captured expenses 2. Recurring subscriptions I should notice 3. Expenses missing receipts 4. Expenses tied to a project or client 5. Anything that needs follow-up 6. One spending pattern worth paying attention to Keep it simple and practical.
