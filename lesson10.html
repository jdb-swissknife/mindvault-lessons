Lesson 10
Decision Log
Stop losing the why behind important choices

The Problem With Decisions in Chat

Agents are great for thinking through options. But if the final decision stays buried in a long conversation, it is hard to find later.

You might remember what you chose, but forget why you chose it.

Today you are building a Decision Log that captures the choice, the reason, the tradeoff, and the next review point.

The Tool You Are Building

Your Decision Log helps with business choices, personal planning, purchases, projects, hiring, offers, content direction, and strategy.

  1. Decision -- what you chose
  2. Options -- what else you considered
  3. Reason -- why this choice won
  4. Tradeoff -- what you are accepting
  5. Review date -- when to revisit it
Step 1

Create Your Decision Log Format

Copy this into your agent.

I want to build a Decision Log. When I say "log decision," ask me for: 1. The decision I made 2. The options I considered 3. Why I chose this option 4. The tradeoff or risk I am accepting 5. The next action 6. The date I should review this decision Then summarize it in a clear decision log entry.
Step 2

Log One Real Decision

Pick something real but not overwhelming. Examples: which offer to focus on, which habit to build, which tool to use, which project to pause.

Log decision. Decision: [what I chose] Options considered: [option A, option B, option C] Reason: [why this choice makes sense] Tradeoff or risk: [what I am accepting] Next action: [what I will do next] Review date: [when to revisit]
Step 3

Use the "Future Me" Test

A good decision log entry should help you six weeks from now.

Weak EntryStrong Entry
Chose the new offerChose the new offer because it has clearer buyer pain and faster proof
Going with tool AGoing with tool A for 30 days because setup is faster, even though tool B may scale better
Pausing projectPausing project until Friday because two client tasks have higher cash impact
Need to follow upFollow up Thursday with a simple yes/no ask because the last reply was positive but vague
Step 4

Ask Your Agent to Challenge the Decision

Before you save a big decision, make the agent pressure-test it.

Challenge this decision before I save it. Tell me: 1. The strongest reason this decision is right 2. The strongest reason it might be wrong 3. What information is missing 4. What early signal would tell me to change course 5. The smallest next step that keeps risk low Then write the final decision log entry.

What's Happening Behind the Scenes

You are teaching your agent how you make decisions. Over time, this helps it spot patterns: where you move fast, where you overthink, and where you need better data.

Safety note: Your agent can help you think. You still own the decision.
Step 5

Your Assignment

Your to-do list:
1. Create the Decision Log format
2. Log one real decision
3. Ask your agent to challenge it
4. Save the final entry
5. Set a review date

The Bigger Picture

A decision log gives your agent a practical memory of your strategy, not just your preferences.

The real takeaway: Capturing the why is what makes a decision useful later.
